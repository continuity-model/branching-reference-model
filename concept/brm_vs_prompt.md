# BRM vs Prompt

## Summary

Prompt influences output.  
BRM structures reasoning.

---

## Key Difference

| Aspect | Prompt | BRM |
|--------|--------|-----|
| Control | Instruction-based | Structure-based |
| Scope | Initial response | Full interaction |
| Stability | Degrades over time | Maintained |
| Context | Implicit | Explicit |
| Behavior | Influenced | Controlled |
| Effort | Minimal | Requires structure |

---

## What Happens

Prompt-based interaction:

- shapes the initial response  
- relies on instructions  
- works well for single-turn tasks  
- gradually loses influence as context grows  

BRM-based interaction:

- structures the entire reasoning flow  
- separates stages (generation, review, evaluation)  
- prevents context mixing  
- maintains consistency across steps  

---

## Where Prompt Works Better

Prompt-based approaches are often better when:

- you need a strong first output  
- the task is simple or well-defined  
- interaction is short  
- you want immediate results without structure  

Prompting is highly effective for one-shot tasks.

---

## Where BRM Works Better

BRM is more effective when:

- reasoning must remain stable across steps  
- multiple contexts need to be handled without mixing  
- decisions require justification  
- outputs must preserve intent  

---

## Insight

Prompt excels at initial generation.  
BRM excels across interaction.

---

## Conclusion

Prompt gives you a good answer.  
BRM lets you keep it good.

They are not replacements.  
They operate at different layers.
