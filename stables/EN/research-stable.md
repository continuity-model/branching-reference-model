# Research Stable

## Problem

Research tasks often begin with a broad question rather than a clearly defined problem.

Examples include:

- exploring an unfamiliar technical topic
- gathering background information for a project
- comparing possible solutions
- investigating an emerging field

Unlike debugging or problem diagnosis, research rarely begins with a single observable failure point.

Instead, the challenge lies in **navigating large volumes of information while maintaining a coherent investigation path**.

AI collaboration introduces an additional difficulty.  
Language models are capable of generating confident explanations even when the available information is incomplete, uncertain, or loosely connected.

As research progresses, the investigation may gradually drift away from its original objective.

This typically appears in three forms of **information drift**:

- **topic drift** — the investigation gradually shifts to loosely related subjects  
- **evidence drift** — speculation begins to mix with confirmed information  
- **scope drift** — the investigation expands beyond its intended boundaries

When these drifts accumulate, the research path becomes difficult to follow and conclusions become unreliable.

Research Stable addresses this problem by enforcing a **structured information exploration workflow** that maintains alignment between the research question, the collected evidence base, and the scope of investigation.

---

## Failure Pattern

Typical failures observed during AI-assisted research include:

- drifting away from the original research question
- mixing confirmed information with speculation
- introducing loosely related topics without clear relevance
- expanding the research scope without clear justification
- prematurely concluding based on incomplete information
- repeatedly exploring similar information without advancing the investigation

These behaviors destabilize research because the investigation path gradually loses structure and clarity.

---

## What This Mode Does

Research Stable introduces an operational discipline focused on **controlled information exploration**.

Instead of allowing research to expand indefinitely, the mode anchors the investigation to three structural references:

- the research question
- the evidence base collected
- the defined scope of the investigation

The workflow explicitly monitors the three common forms of information drift:

- topic drift
- evidence drift
- scope drift

By maintaining awareness of these drift patterns, Research Stable keeps the investigation aligned with the original objective.

The mode prioritizes **evidence over narrative**, ensuring that conclusions are derived from **verifiable evidence** rather than plausible explanations.

---

## Expected Improvements

Using Research Stable improves collaboration stability by:

- maintaining focus on the original research question
- preventing uncontrolled expansion of research topics
- separating confirmed information from speculation
- preserving clarity about the scope of the investigation
- supporting evidence-based conclusions

As a result, research sessions remain structured and easier to navigate.

---

## Operational Structure

The reasoning workflow enforced by Research Stable follows a structured research sequence.

Step 1 — Define the research question  
Clarify what is being investigated.

Step 2 — Define the investigation scope  
Determine the boundaries of the research.

Step 3 — Collect relevant information  
Gather information directly related to the research question.

Step 4 — Separate evidence from interpretation  
Distinguish confirmed information from assumptions or speculation.

Step 5 — Synthesize findings  
Form conclusions supported by the collected **evidence base**.

This structure keeps research aligned with the original objective while preventing information drift.
