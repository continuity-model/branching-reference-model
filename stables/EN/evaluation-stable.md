# Evaluation Stable

## Problem

Evaluation tasks require making judgments based on defined criteria.

Typical evaluation contexts include:

- comparing alternative solutions
- assessing design quality
- reviewing documents or proposals
- determining whether requirements are satisfied

Unlike exploratory tasks such as research or writing, evaluation requires a **stable reference framework** for judging quality or correctness.

If evaluation criteria are unclear or inconsistent, conclusions can become arbitrary.

AI collaboration introduces additional instability during evaluation.  
Language models tend to produce persuasive explanations even when evaluation standards are not explicitly defined.

As a result, discussions may gradually shift their evaluation criteria during the conversation.

This typically leads to inconsistent judgments, premature conclusions, or decisions based on subjective impressions rather than defined standards.

Evaluation Stable addresses this problem by enforcing a **criteria-anchored evaluation workflow**.

---

## Failure Pattern

Typical failures observed during AI-assisted evaluation include:

- drawing conclusions before evaluation criteria are defined
- changing evaluation standards during the discussion
- mixing subjective preference with objective criteria
- evaluating different options using inconsistent standards
- emphasizing persuasive explanations instead of measurable criteria
- reaching conclusions without systematic comparison

These behaviors destabilize evaluation because judgments lose their objective reference point.

---

## What This Mode Does

Evaluation Stable introduces an operational discipline focused on **criteria-grounded judgment**.

Instead of allowing conclusions to emerge from narrative persuasion, the mode anchors evaluation to explicit criteria.

The workflow emphasizes three structural references:

- defined evaluation criteria
- consistent comparison standards
- transparent justification of conclusions

By maintaining these anchors, Evaluation Stable prevents evaluation discussions from drifting into subjective reasoning.

The mode prioritizes **criteria over persuasion**, ensuring that conclusions are derived from explicit standards rather than narrative arguments.

---

## Expected Improvements

Using Evaluation Stable improves collaboration stability by:

- preventing premature judgments
- maintaining consistent evaluation criteria
- separating subjective preference from objective standards
- improving transparency in decision making
- supporting **consistent and fair comparison** between alternatives

As a result, evaluation becomes more systematic and easier to justify.

---

## Operational Structure

The reasoning workflow enforced by Evaluation Stable follows a structured evaluation sequence.

Step 1 — Define evaluation criteria  
Establish the standards used to judge alternatives.

Step 2 — Identify the options  
Clarify what alternatives are being evaluated.

Step 3 — Apply the criteria  
Assess each option using the defined standards.

Step 4 — Compare results  
Evaluate the relative strengths and weaknesses.

Step 5 — Derive the conclusion  
Reach a conclusion supported by the evaluation criteria.

This structure ensures that evaluation decisions remain grounded in explicit standards.
