# Continuity Model

Designing structures for stable human–AI collaboration.

Most AI discussions focus on making models smarter or writing better prompts.  
My work focuses on something different: **stability in long-form collaboration with AI.**

When conversations become longer and more complex, AI interaction often collapses due to structural issues:

- role drift  
- shallow reasoning  
- generic feedback  
- unnecessary explanation  
- context instability  
- tool inconsistency  

I build frameworks and operational modes designed to reduce those failures and make AI collaboration more reliable.

---

## Why I started building this

I originally wanted a space where I could think together with AI.

At first, it worked surprisingly well. Ideas flowed naturally and conversations expanded in multiple directions.

But when I tried to stabilize the collaboration by tightening rules and constraints, the space suddenly collapsed.

That experience revealed something important.

The problem was not AI intelligence.

The real problem was **continuity**.

Humans experience conversation as a flowing timeline.  
But inside the model, interaction is reconstructed from scattered references.

Continuity was never a timeline.  
It was always **relational structure**.

The Branching Reference Model (BRM) started from that realization.

---

## What I'm building

### Branching Reference Model (BRM)

A structural framework for maintaining **reference continuity** in long AI collaboration.

Instead of treating conversations as linear history, BRM treats them as a network of references.

---

### Stable Thinking Stack

A layered model for structured reasoning during AI collaboration.

It separates:

- reference structure  
- reasoning discipline  
- task-specific behavior  

---

### Stable Modes

Governance-based operational modes that help AI behave more consistently during real work.

Examples include:

- Writing Stable  
- Review Stable  
- Debug Stable  
- Research Stable  
- Spec Stable  
- Legal Stable  
- Symptom Stable  

These are not prompt templates.  
They are **behavioral governance layers**.

---

## Long-term vision

The goal is not to keep patching AI from the outside forever.

The long-term goal is **reference-aware AI** that can maintain:

- scope continuity  
- structured collaboration  
- stable reasoning environments  

BRM and Stable Modes are bridge technologies toward that future.
